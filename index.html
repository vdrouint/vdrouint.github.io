Hello!

I'm Victor Drouin-Touchette, a Ph.D. candidate at Rutgers University in theoretical condensed matter physics. I work in Prof. Piers Coleman 's group. I work on analytical and numerical approaches of competing phases in many-body physics. I expect to graduate in July 2022, and will start as a Postdoctoral Reseacher in Prof. Ananda Roy's group at Rutgers University in September 2022, with a focus on many-body perspectives in quantum computing.

From 2013 to 2016, I was an undergraduate student in physics and mathematics at the Université de Montréal. During that time, I was also a research intern at University of Waterloo and at Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology in frustrated magnetism.

Research

My research in theoretical condensed matter physics focuses on analytical and numerical approaches to strongly correlated phenomena. The main thrust of my thesis is devoted to the study of electron correlations in iron-based superconductors, as driven by the local physics of the iron atoms. I aim to understand how the development of quantum entanglement within multiorbital systems leads to electron pairing in the bulk. A complementary component to my research covers the study of hidden order in the context of classical statistical mechanics. This involves the study of coupled XY models, used to describe the melting of nematic liquid crystals. These studies lead to the proposal of confinement of fractional vortices, while also explaining an enduring experimental puzzle.
