Hello!

I'm Victor Drouin-Touchette, a 6th year Graduate Student at Rutgers University. I work within Prof. Piers Coleman 's group. I expect to graduate in May 2022, and am currently looking for a Postdoctoral opportunity in the US.

From 2013 to 2016 I was an undergraduate student in Physics and Mathematics at the Université de Montréal. During that time, I worked on frustrated magnetism as a research intern at University of Waterloo and at Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology.

Research

My research in theoretical condensed matter physics focuses on analytical and numerical approaches to strongly correlated phenomena. The main thrust of my thesis is devoted to the study of electron correlation in iron-based superconductors, as driven by the local physics of the iron atoms. I aim to understand how the development of quantum entanglement within multiorbital systems leads to electron pairing in the bulk. A complementary component to my research covers the study of hidden order in the context of classical statistical mechanics. This involves the study of coupled XY models, used to describe the melting of nematic liquid crystals. These studies lead to the proposal of confinement of fractional vortices, while also explaining an enduring experimental puzzle.
